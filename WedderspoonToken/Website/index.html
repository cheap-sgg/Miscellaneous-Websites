Tokenomics

50% initial burn

Alot of developers will tell you that the reason to burn a huge amount of the token supply is to make the token more scarce. This is a blatant lie because the token issuer could have just made less tokens to begin with. The real reason the dev burns that many tokens is because of the buyer not wanting to see certain wallets holding massive token portions and an easy way to circumvent this problem is by sending a huge amount of the supply to a dead wallet.

no swap fees

Wedderspoon was designed to have a very low slippage tolerance. This means that there is no swap fee mechanism implemented. AKA 0% slippage. Due to price volatility though, occasionally slippage may need to be bumped up to a litle over 0%. e.g. 0.01%, 0.001%.

Locked Liquidity

All LP tokens were sent to a timelocked smart contract here.
